How to Care for Denim (and Why You Shouldn't Wash It Often)

Raw selvedge denim is woven tighter and dyed differently than the stretch denim most people are used to, which is part of why it fades the way it does. The indigo sits on the surface of each yarn rather than soaking all the way through, so the more a pair is worn and bent,s at the knees, the back pockets, the ankles, the more that surface dye wears away and the underlying white core shows through.

Washing resets that process, which is why most denim heads wait three to six months before a first wash, and even then favour a cold soak over a machine cycle. We size our Selvedge Straight Jean to account for the slight shrinkage that comes with that first wash, so buy true to your usual size rather than sizing up.

If you do need to spot-clean before then, a damp cloth and a little dish soap on the stain is usually enough, and it won't disturb the fade pattern building up everywhere else.

Repair, Don't Replace: Inside Our Mending Program

Every Drift piece ships with a small repair card that lists a code printed inside the garment. If a seam goes, a button comes off, or a pocket tears, mail it back with that code and our workshop will mend it at no cost, for as long as you own the piece, not just within a return window.

Most repairs take under a week. Joe, who handles patterns and production, also keeps a small log of which failure points come up most often, which has fed back into two pattern changes so far, including a reinforced chest pocket on the current Heavyweight Pocket Tee.

Want to send something in? Message us with your repair code and a photo, and we'll send a prepaid label.
